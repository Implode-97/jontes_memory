# Learning: Root-cause analysis must continue past the first visible failure chain

## Context
This came from a Terragrunt E2E cleanup investigation where I stopped after identifying a plausible downstream failure chain instead of proving the earliest fundamental trigger in the raw CI log and surrounding code.

## Learning
For this user, bug and CI investigations must always go to the most fundamental flaw that can be supported from full logs, code, and configuration. It is not enough to explain cleanup fallout, ordering symptoms, or the last visible error if there may be a deeper trigger earlier in the run. The user explicitly prefers spending more tokens, time, repeated passes, and even subagents to avoid patchwork fixes and wack-a-mole debugging.

## Future Use
When investigating failures, keep tracing until the first real failing operation and its underlying contract or permission problem are identified. Use raw job logs, filtering tools, code reading, and subagents as needed. Do not stop at downstream cleanup errors or partial causal chains unless the user explicitly asks for quick triage only.
