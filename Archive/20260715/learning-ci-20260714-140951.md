# Learning: MR 32 green pipelines depend on janitor recovery

## Context
GitLab traces for data-platform-terragrunt-catalog MR !32 were compared with MR !33 and subsequent master runs while investigating intermittent-looking S3 BucketNotEmpty cleanup failures.

## Learning
MR !33 pipeline 7952027/job 36145867 was the last MR pipeline observed to complete the canonical nine-unit Terragrunt root destroy without invoking janitor. Master pipelines for the same commit also destroyed cleanly, including pipeline 7961525/job 36208177 on 2026-07-10. Every successful MR !32 E2E pipeline inspected, including its earliest successful job 35753604, invoked janitor after root destroy failed. Earlier MR !32 failures were Terragrunt EOF/early-exit failures; recent ten-unit runs reach dev workspace deletion and fail with BucketNotEmpty. Therefore green MR !32 status demonstrates successful recovery, not a clean primary destroy.

## Future Use
When reviewing MR !32, distinguish pipeline success from root-destroy success. Treat the SQL warehouse as the strongest correlated topology change, but require S3 object-key/timestamp or CloudTrail data-event evidence before identifying it as the writer.
