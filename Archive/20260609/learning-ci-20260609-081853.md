# Learning: Keep AI Review CI Least-Privilege

## Context
While scoring `data-platform-terragrunt-catalog/ci/codex-review.yml`, the CI structure was mostly sound but the invoked Codex runtime had broader operational and security exposure than necessary.

## Learning
Optional AI review lanes can be asynchronous and non-authoritative, but they still need least-privilege runtime boundaries. Avoid unpinned CLI installs in CI, avoid passing broad `CI_`, `AWS_`, token, and proxy environments into the AI subprocess, and keep GitLab read/write tokens isolated to small check/post helpers. If sandbox bypass is required for Codex review, treat that as a deliberate risk that should be constrained by environment filtering and reproducible tooling.

## Future Use
When reviewing AI-assisted CI jobs, score GitLab semantics separately from runtime safety. Reward `needs: []`, explicit non-blocking `allow_failure`, and serialized first-review behavior, but penalize unpinned tools, broad env forwarding, and missing concurrency guards for manual reruns.
